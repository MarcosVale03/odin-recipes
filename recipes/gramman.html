Antonio's Gramman

Description:

Steamed bread stuffed with Japanese chestnuts and azuki beans is a delightful Japanese confection, known as "kuri an manju" in Japanese. The soft, pillowy steamed bread, or "manju," encases a sweet and earthy filling made from tender chestnuts and a sweet red bean paste known as "azuki."

Ingredients:

  • 120g flour
  • 60g sugar
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 egg
  • Milk, roughly 120ml
  • 160g Tsubu anko
  • 4 candied chestnuts

Steps:

  1. Cut candied chestnuts in half. Place egg in a bowl.
  2. Add milk until the liquid weighs 120mL. Add sugar and vegetable oil, and mix well.
  3. Vigorously stir flour and baking powder into bowl. Beat with an eggbeater/whisk until all flour is mixed
  4. Using a spoon, fill paper cups halfway with mixture. Evenly divide anko and place a spoonful in the middle of each cup
  5. Place 1 chestnut piece per cup.Place cups in steamer. Put on the lid, and steam on low for 10 minutes.
  6. Poke with bamboo skewers, when nothing sticks, they are done.

Enjoy!!
